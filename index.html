Mission

Photography
from the
street up.

Pavement Photos is a photography collective rooted in the documentary and street traditions. Urban, wild, social, environmental — unposed, unglamorous.

We make photographs that bear honest witness to the world and the forces that shape both. We work with organisations who want their story told truthfully.

We are on the side of people over profit, place over development, the overlooked over the celebrated.

Our
Story.


We draw on a long tradition of photographers who pointed their cameras at the real world — from the earliest Scottish documentary work to the cooperative model pioneered by Magnum Photos.

We bring together photographers working in the documentary and street traditions to share work, develop practice, and produce photography that matters.

We are on the side of people over profit, place over development, the overlooked over the celebrated.

Our
Principles.

  • The collective will not accept commissions that conflict with its values.
  • Members retain full copyright and ownership of all work at all times.
  • Decisions affecting the collective are made by consensus among active members.
  • New members are invited by existing members based on quality and ethos of work.
  • Any commercial income is distributed transparently and agreed collectively.

Monthly
Meetup

We meet once a month in Perth. A projector, some pints, and honest conversation about photography and the work we're making. Attendance at monthly meetings is open to anyone with a genuine interest in the documentary and street traditions.

Membership is selective rather than open. Prospective members show work and are invited in by existing members. This is how we maintain a shared standard and a coherent collective voice.
